StimASD: A fine-grained dataset and balanced patch-partition embedding approach towards self-stimulated behavior recognition in autism

We construct a fine-grained dataset of self-stimulatory behaviors in autism, termed StimASD. This video dataset contains 8 categories and 520 clips, with fine annotation and diverse samples. A total of 202 videos are obtained, of which 27 originated from the SSBD dataset. To ensure fine-grained data quality, these videos are segmented to contain only a single behavior category. Each video clip is annotated in 'StimASD.xlsx', including the occurrence time, duration, body parts involved in the action, and the number of individuals.
1 - Arm flapping;
2 - Finger flicking;
3 - Head banging;
4 - Spinning;
5 - Toe walking;
6 - Beating oneself;
7 - Rocking back and forth;
8 - Hand biting;
